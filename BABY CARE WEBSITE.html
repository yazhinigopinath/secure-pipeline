Baby Food Schedule

Age-appropriate feeding guidelines from newborn to 2 years old

0-6 Months

  • Exclusive breastfeeding or formula
  • Feed every 2-3 hours (8-12 times/day)
  • No solids or water needed
  • Watch for hunger cues like rooting, sucking

6-12 Months

  • Begin iron-rich foods (pureed meats, cereals)
  • Introduce one new food every 3-5 days
  • Breastmilk or formula still primary nutrition
  • Progress to mashed then finger foods

12-24 Months

  • Transition to whole milk (16-24 oz/day)
  • 3 meals + 2-3 snacks per day
  • Offer variety from all food groups
  • Encourage self-feeding with utensils

Important: These are general guidelines. Always consult with your pediatrician about your baby's specific nutritional needs.

0-3 Months Milestones

Newborn

Physical

  • Moves arms and legs symmetrically
  • Lifts head briefly during tummy time
  • Holds head up when held upright

Communication

  • Makes cooing sounds
  • Cries differently for different needs
  • Quiets or smiles in response to voice

Cognitive

  • Recognizes familiar voices
  • Tracks objects with eyes
  • Shows interest in faces

Remember: All babies develop at their own pace. These milestones show what most babies can do by certain ages. If you have any concerns about your baby's development, consult your pediatrician.

Parenting Tips & Resources

Essential Tips for New Parents

Sleep Strategies

Newborns sleep 14-17 hours a day in 1-3 hour stretches. Create a calming bedtime routine with dim lights, soft music, and swaddling to help establish good sleep habits.

Soothing Techniques

Try the 5 S's: Swaddle, Side/Stomach position (while awake), Shush, Swing, and Suck. White noise machines can also help calm a fussy baby by mimicking the womb.

When to Call the Doctor

Call immediately for: fever (rectal temp ≥100.4°F in babies <3 months), difficulty breathing, no wet diapers for 6+ hours, or inconsolable crying for prolonged periods.
